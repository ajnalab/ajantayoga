Guided audio and video sessions — freely available. Begin wherever you are. All sessions can be practiced seated, lying down, or in a chair.

Prana means vital energy or life force — the force which exists in all things, whether animate or inanimate. Although closely related to the breath, prana is more subtle than air or oxygen.

Pranayama utilises breathing to influence the flow of prana through the energy channels (nadis) of the subtle body, ultimately affecting mind, emotion, and consciousness. The simplest practice — observing the natural breath — is also the most profound.

Natural Breath Awareness

Balancing Practices

Working with the solar and lunar channels to restore equilibrium in body and mind.

All levels · Free

Nadi Shodhana — Alternate Nostril

Balancing Ida and Pingala — the lunar and solar channels. Deeply calming, clarifying, and harmonising. The most widely recommended pranayama in the classical tradition.

⏱ 20 min · Can be practiced with one hand if needed

Intermediate

Bhramari — Humming Bee Breath

The resonant vibration of a gentle humming sound on the exhalation. Profoundly calming for the nervous system. Excellent for anxiety, insomnia, and headaches.

⏱ 12 min · Particularly effective for stress

Intermediate

Sheetali — Cooling Breath

Inhaling through a rolled or slightly parted tongue to cool and calm the system. Excellent for overheating, inflammation, and fiery emotional states.

⏱ 10 min

Energy & vitality

Energising Practices

Use these in the morning or when energy is low. Not recommended in the evening.

Intermediate · Caution*

Kapalabhati — Skull-Shining Breath

Rapid, rhythmic exhalations to energise and cleanse. Stimulates the digestive fire, clears the respiratory system, and vitalises the mind.

⏱ 15 min

*Not suitable for high blood pressure, pregnancy, or heart conditions.

All levels

Box Breathing — Equal Ratio

Inhale, retain, exhale, retain — in equal counts. A deeply stabilising practice that balances the nervous system and builds breath capacity gently.

⏱ 15 min · Suitable for most conditions

Guided meditation

Yoga Nidra — Yogic Sleep

A guided practice of deep relaxation and conscious rest. Neither asleep nor fully awake. Said to be as restorative as several hours of sleep. Excellent for fatigue, pain, and healing.

⏱ 30 min · Always practiced lying down
