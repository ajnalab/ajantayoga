About Ajanta Yoga Academy

A sanctuary for those who seek yoga beyond the posture — as a philosophy, a breath practice, and a way of living.

Our mission

Yoga belongs to everyone

Founded on the conviction that the deepest gifts of yoga are available to all bodies, all ages, and all conditions.

Ajanta Yoga Academy takes its name from the Ajanta Caves of Maharashtra — ancient repositories of Buddhist art and philosophy, carved from living rock over centuries. Like those caves, we believe wisdom accumulates slowly, through patient dedication, and that the most profound teachings are often the most quietly held.

Our approach is non-sectarian, grounded in classical texts, and always adapted to the living reality of the practitioner before us. We work with the body you have today, not the one you imagine you should have.

🪷

"The yoga of understanding"

Our practice draws equally from Jnana (knowledge), Bhakti (devotion), and Pranayama — with asana in service of the whole, not as the goal itself.

Our approach

Yoga for practitioners with physical limitations

Injury and illness do not disqualify you from yoga. They may be your deepest invitation into it.

The physical postures of yoga (asana) are one limb of eight on Patanjali's path. When the body cannot perform the full asana, the other seven limbs remain fully available: breath, ethical living, sensory withdrawal, concentration, and meditation.

Our adaptive courses are designed with this understanding. We work with: chronic pain conditions, post-surgical recovery, neurological conditions, arthritis and joint conditions, fatigue-related conditions (ME/CFS, fibromyalgia), anxiety and stress-related conditions, and age-related limitations.

Every course includes modifications, alternatives, and a strong emphasis on the breath and philosophical teachings — so that even in sessions when movement is minimal, the practice is complete.

"Sthira sukham asanam — the posture should be steady and comfortable."

— Yoga Sutras of Patanjali, II.46. The standard applies to all of life, not only the mat.

Roots

Our philosophical lineage

Ajanta Yoga Academy draws from the classical Indian philosophical traditions, approached with scholarly rigour and contemplative sincerity:

📜

Classical Yoga

The Yoga Sutras of Patanjali as a complete psychological and spiritual science. We study all eight limbs, with particular depth on the inner limbs (Dharana, Dhyana, Samadhi).

🕉️

Vedanta & Tantra

Advaita Vedanta, the Bhagavad Gita, and Tantric philosophy inform our understanding of the self, consciousness, and the nature of practice itself.

☸️

Buddhist Contemplative Tradition

The rich crossover between yoga and Buddhist meditation practice enriches our approach to mindfulness, equanimity, and the study of the mind.

🌬️

Hatha Yoga

The classical Hatha tradition — breath, energy locks (bandha), and posture — is explored as a vehicle for inner transformation, not aesthetic achievement.
